Prajna AI Presents MAFIA Framework at ICML 2025, Advances Multi-Agent Systems for Financial Services

Prajna Inc. contributed to the International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML) 2025, with Dr. Ramesh Sarukkai serving as a panelist at the Workshop on Multi-Agent Systems (MAS) and the team presenting research on self-healing AI agents at the Workshop on Collaborative and Federated Agentic Workflows (CFAgentic).

Contributing to Multi-Agent Intelligence Research

At the MAS workshop, Dr. Ramesh Sarukkai joined AI researchers from Google DeepMind, University of Washington, and other academic institutions in a panel discussion exploring how collaborative AI systems can enhance enterprise applications. The workshop focused on how scaling agent collaboration could expand capabilities in AI systems.

"Our work at ICML 2025 represents an important step for Prajna and the AI community strengthening responsible AI solutions for Finance," saidDr. Ramesh Sarukkai, Founder and CEO of Prajna Inc. "The MAFIA framework we presented demonstrates that multi-agent systems are practical solutions that can help financial institutions operate more effectively while maintaining compliance standards. We're moving from AI that assists to AI that actively protects and improves itself."

MAFIA: Self-Healing AI for Finance

At the CFAgentic workshop, Prajna's research team, led by Arya Sarukkai, Shaohui Sun, and Wei Dai, presented their paper "Multiple Automated Finance Integration Agents (MAFIA) With Self-Healing." The framework addresses the challenge of deploying autonomous AI in regulated financial environments.

Key Features: Self-Healing Through Agent Collaboration

The MAFIA framework employs multiple specialized agents that work together to support accuracy and compliance:

  • Automated Compliance Checking: gents review each other's responses for regulatory adherence
  • Rubric-Based Scoring: A 100-point system evaluates responses across consumer protection, privacy, ethics, and operational risk
  • Real-Time Refinement: Self-healing mechanisms that improve responses without human intervention{" "}

In testing with real-world lending scenarios, the framework achieved a 95.45% reduction in compliance violations using the self-healing rubric system and reached an average score of 93.55 on financial compliance metrics. These results demonstrate the potential of multi-agent approaches in financial services applications.

Practical Applications

The MAFIA framework offers new options for financial institutions looking to implement AI while maintaining regulatory compliance. By enabling agents to monitor and improve each other autonomously, the system aims to reduce risk while supporting operational efficiency.{" "}

"We're seeing an evolution in how AI can be deployed," added Dr. Sarukkai during the MAS panel discussion. "Financial institutions can work toward both innovation and compliance. Our multi-agent approach helps ensure both objectives can be addressed together."
